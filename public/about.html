What is Tuberculosis?

Tuberculosis (TB) is a disease caused by bacteria called Mycobacterium tuberculosis. The bacteria usually attacks the lungs, but they can also damage other parts of the body. TB is one of the top 10 causes of death and the leading cause from a single infectious agent (above HIV/AIDS).

How does Tuberculosis spreads?

TB is spread from person to person through the air. When people with lung TB are caught, sneezed or spit, they propel the TB germs into the air. A person needs to inhale only a few of these grams to become infected. Although TB is spread in a similar way to a cold or flu, it is not as contagious. You would have to spend prolonged periods in close contact with an infected person to catch the infection yourself.

Signs and Symptoms

Latent TB doesn’t have symptoms, but signs can be found using TB skin or blood test Signs of active TB disease include:

Major

  • Bad cough for 3 weeks or longer
  • Chest Pain
  • Coughing up blood
  • Coughing up sputum (phlegm within the lungs)

Minor

  • Feeling tired all the time
  • Night sweats
  • Fatigue
  • Chills
  • Fever
  • Loss of appetite
  • Weight loss

Types of Tuberculosis

There are difference between disease and infecions:

Spreading of TB bacteria Cannot infect others May infect others

Prevention

There are several methods to prevent TB transmission:

  • Bacillus Calmette-Guérin (BCG) vaccine
  • TB education towards TB patients
  • Use of masks and respirators
  • Precaution action cough etiquette

Treatment

There are a few types of treatment for TB:

  • (Latent TB) Using Isoniazid antibiotic pill daily for at least 9 months as ordered by the doctor. However, Rifampin is an alternative antibiotic pill to be taken daily for at least 4 months.
  • (TB Disease) There are four medications used for this which are Ethambutol, Isoniazid, Pyrazinamide, and Rifampin. The doctor will run a test on the patient to determine which antibiotics is effective towards the TB bacteria.
  • (Drug-Resistant TB) Some of the TB bacteria is mutated and hence they are resistant to antibiotics. New antibiotic is introduced to cure this but takes longer time which is Fluoroquinolones. Other medications are introduced as well such as Amikacin, Capreomycin, Kanamycin, Bedaquiline, Ethionamide, and Para-amino salicylic acid. Scientists are still studying these medicines.
